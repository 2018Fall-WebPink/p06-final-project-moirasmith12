Mary Jackson:

Mathematician, Aerospace Engineer

Quick Facts

  • Birthday: April 9th, 1921
  • Hometown: Hampton, VA
  • Education: Hampton Institute
  • Died: February 11, 2005
  • Employed at NASA: 1951-1985
  • Hidden Figures: The newly released movie, Hidden Figures, shed light on three intelligent African American women who were key behind-the-scene workers at NASA, including Mary Jackson.

Main Content

Mary Jackson was born in Hampton, VA in 1921, during a time of racial segregation. Right away, she excelled academically, specifically in the math and science fields. Her acadamic excellence soon earned her the postition of "human computer" for NASA. Of course, that was after earning dual bachelor's degrees, mathematics and physical science, from Hampton Institute.

Her accomplishments are truly amazing for any person, but during this time, Mary Jackson wasn't just a normal person. She was an African American woman. While working for NASA, she certainly encountered the effects of segregation, like having to eat at her desk, or going to a "colored" bathroom. After dealing with these unfair accomadations for several months, Jackson was having enough and considered stepping down from her position. That's when she had a "chance encounter" with a supervisor, who, after hearing Jackson's complaints, saw her potential and hired her to work for him. After taking engineering classes, Jackson was promoted to NASA's first black female engineer. She paved the way for others to follow, and because of her courage and strength in tough times, she inspires so many each and every day.
